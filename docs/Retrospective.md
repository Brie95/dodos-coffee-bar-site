## 1. Overview  
This retrospective reflects on my experience planning, building, and delivering the Dodo’s Coffee Bar website. The project was simple in scope, but it still required organization, decision‑making, and steady progress. Looking back, I can clearly see what worked well, what challenged me, and what I would do differently next time.

---

## 2. What Went Well  

### **Clear Scope and Direction**  
Finalizing the scope early made the rest of the project feel manageable. Knowing I only needed two pages kept me focused and prevented me from drifting into unnecessary features.

### **Consistent Visual Style**  
Choosing a warm, cozy color palette helped everything fall into place. Once the brand vibe was set, writing the content and styling the pages felt natural.

### **Using Loops (Iterative Work)**  
Working in small loops—structure → styling → content → refinement—kept me from getting stuck. Each pass made the site cleaner and more polished without overwhelming me.

### **GitHub Workflow**  
Using GitHub Desktop made version control easier than I expected. Committing changes as I went helped me stay organized and confident that nothing would get lost.

---

## 3. What Was Challenging  

### **Getting Files in the Right Place**  
At first, GitHub Desktop didn’t show my changes because my files weren’t in the correct folder. It took a moment to understand how the repo folder and my local files needed to match.

### **Styling Details**  
CSS can be picky, and small spacing or alignment issues took longer than expected. I had to remind myself not to chase perfection too early.

### **Balancing Creativity and Requirements**  
I wanted the site to feel personal and cozy, but I also had to stay within the assignment’s technical limits. Finding that balance took some intentional decision‑making.

---

## 4. What I Would Do Differently Next Time  

### **Start the Documentation Earlier**  
Even though the website itself was straightforward, the documentation took time. Next time, I would write each section as I go instead of saving it for the end.

### **Sketch the Layout First**  
A quick sketch or wireframe would have saved me time when building the HTML structure. Visualizing the layout before coding would make the process smoother.

### **Commit More Frequently**  
I committed regularly, but I could have committed even more often with smaller changes. It helps with tracking progress and makes troubleshooting easier.

---

## 5. Key Lessons Learned  

- **Small loops keep the project moving.**  
- **A clear scope prevents overwhelm.**  
- **Branding decisions early on make everything else easier.**  
- **GitHub is much simpler once the folder structure is correct.**  
- **Good documentation is part of the project, not an afterthought.**

---

## 6. Final Reflection  
Overall, I’m proud of how this project came together. It reflects my style—warm, welcoming, and intentional—and it shows that I can take a project from planning to delivery with clarity and follow‑through.  

This wasn’t just about building a website; it was about practicing real project management skills in a way that felt creative and personal. I walked away with more confidence in my ability to organize work, manage risks, and deliver something polished.
